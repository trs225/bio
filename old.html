projects

It's common to observe that, while promising to connect us, social media etc. also do the opposite, mass-producing hyper-individuality in an extractive process that separates us both virtually and in real life.

This page exists as a starting point for thinking about what else might be possible if focus is placed instead on what people share in common with each other.

As you click around the page, you see the footprints of the visitors who came here before you, and you contibute your own. These show how it has been used by others and how you might use it yourself.

Though here these footprints are just generated at random, they're meant to suggest how people might passively teach each other how to use a simple website like this one.

More generally it's hoped to suggest how systems that focus on people's commonality and lived experiences of participating in them might be shaped to work better for people collectively by fostering the pursuit of their common, de-individualized interests.
