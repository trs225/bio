It's common to observe that, while promising to connect us, social media etc. also do the opposite, mass-producing hyper-individuality in an extractive process that separates us both virtually and "in real life."

This page exists as an experiment to explore what else might be possible if focus is placed instead on what's shared in common, as a starting point for thinking about how people can learn to act collaboratively, and as a place to put my resume.

As you click around the page, you see the footprints of the people who came here before you, and you contibute your own. These show how it has been used by others and how you might use it yourself.

Though here these footprints are just generated at random, hopefully they demonstrate how a site like this might lend itself to its own reorganization as visual data accrue.

More generally, it is hoped to suggest how systems that focus on people's commonality and lived experiences of participating in them might be shaped to work better for people collectively by fostering the pursuit of their common, de-individualized interests.
